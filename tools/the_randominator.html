how to ×
Want to do something creative but are stuck on "What do I do?" Just check all the boxes you want as input and click "Gimme something". See something you like that doesn't fit with the rest? Lock it and continue. But beware: There's no going back, so take your time to read the prompts, or just spam it and let randomness decide. If you like a color that appears, hover over it to see all the details or click to copy the hex code. Want to save something for later? Just download it. :)

Some Possibly Helpful Tips

For the style category, you can find plenty of visual inspiration on a certain app/website that starts with P and rhymes with Binterest. If a word appears that you don't recognize, simply google it!

You can interpret prompts literally, but no one's stopping you from thinking outside the box. For example, take something simple like "a door." You could use the first door that comes to mind, or you could brainstorm:

  • A door is wooden, but it can also be made of glass.
  • There are push doors, pull doors, rotating doors, and sliding doors.
  • Doors need hinges and a doorknob.
  • "Eyes are the door to the soul," and an open door can symbolize a new opportunity.
  • There are doors for specific uses: fire doors, pet doors, ...

And so on and so forth.

about ×

The idea for this design tool originated a while ago, as I wanted to prompt myself to do more creative things outside of university, or just in general rather than my usual doomscrolling. But I was still left with the question: "What do I do... ?" So, I downloaded a wheel-spin app and created the first version of my inspiration generator, which became the foundation for the tool you see now.

I added even more words to each category and included a color generator, giving myself – and anyone in need of a little inspiration – a great starting point for some creativity.

Since this tool is based on a subjective brainstorm, some references may not be familiar to everyone, but they're definitely doable with a quick Google search. Important side note: This is purely based on words I thought would be interesting for projects, and I am in no way affiliated with any brands or products mentioned.
